## Who Was Lalon?

Lalon Shah (c. 1774–1890) was a mystic saint, philosopher, and songwriter whose compositions form the backbone of Baul music in Bengal.

## Core Philosophy

Lalon's songs reject rigid religious identity in favor of a search for the "Moner Manush" (the person of the heart) — a symbol of self-realization and divine unity.

## Legacy

His shrine in Kushtia, Bangladesh, remains a pilgrimage site for Baul practitioners, and his songs continue to be reinterpreted by contemporary folk artists.
